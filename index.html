TRANQUIL HAVEN OF BADOC

Badoc, a coastal municipality in Ilocos Norte, Philippines, has a population of 32,530 people spread across 31 barangays. The town's name comes from a story in 1572 when Spanish missionaries established camp near the mouth of the Badoc River. The natives, known as the Isneg, occupied the area around the settlement, which was named after a plant called bado-badoc. The town aims to become a tourist destination, focusing on agro-based development and eco-cultural community. Its economy is primarily agricultural, with farming and fishing being its main industries.

Things To Know About Badoc

Badoc Island is an uninhabited beach in Badoc, Ilocos Norte, Philippines, located about 1 km off the shore of Barangay Pagsanahan. It offers a relaxing alternative to other beaches in the area. To reach Badoc Island, take a bus bound for Laoag and then a tricycle ride to La Virgen Milagrosa Cove. The island is bare, uninhabited, and uncommercialized, with no establishments, only transfer boats and small nipa huts. Overnight stays are not allowed.
