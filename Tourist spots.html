Breathtaking Vista

Badoc Island in Ilocos Norte is a hidden gem that offers a breathtaking vista. This privately-owned island is located 25 minutes away from the La Virgen Milagrosa Cove, which is situated at Sitio Pagetpet, Brgy. La Milagrosa in Badoc, Ilocos Norte. The island has a long stretch of white-sand beach that you can enjoy to yourself, allowing you to experience some peace and quiet. Unlike other beach destinations in the country, Badoc Island is not commercialized and has fewer tourists.

The island is bare and does not have any hotels, inns, or similar structures on the beach. You can pitch a tent by the beach or place a picnic blanket under the trees that line the shore. Although the island doesn’t have any structure on it, a lot of activities can be enjoyed. The island also has a lot of Instagrammable spots.

Gabut Norte Beach

Gabut Norte is a barangay in the municipality of Badoc, in the province of Ilocos Norte, Philippines. It has a population of 1,680 as of the 2020 Census. The Gobernadora Mansion Ruins is a popular tourist attraction in Gabut Norte. It was built in the early 1900s and was once a grand mansion owned by a Spanish governor’s wife. Another attraction in Gabut Norte is the Gobernadora Island which is known for its beautiful underwater scenery and is a great spot for diving.

La Virgen Milagrosa Church

La Virgen Milagrosa Church is a Catholic church located in Soriano Street, Barangay Garreta, Badoc, Ilocos Norte, Philippines. It was built in 1930 and is known for its historical significance and unique architecture. The church houses the Miraculous Statue of the Blessed Virgin Mary, which is venerated by many Catholics in the region.

Luna Park

Luna Park is a park in Municipality of Badoc, Ilocos Norte, Ilocos. Luna Park is situated nearby to the sports venue FEM Sports and Cultural Center and the town hall Badoc Municipal Hall.

Floating-Cottage of La virgen milagrosa

The floating cottages in La Virgen Milagrosa, located in Barangay Paguetpet, Badoc, Ilocos Norte, Philippines, are made of bamboos and hays with plastic drums as their floaters. The maximum capacity per floating cottage is 15-20 persons. Rentals for the floating cottages are about P500 for every 4 hours, and P100 for every one-hour extension.
